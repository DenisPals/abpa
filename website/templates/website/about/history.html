Our History

Three decades of serving Antigua & Barbuda's pensioners

Our Journey

1995 - The Beginning

A group of retired government workers came together to form the Antigua and Barbuda Pensioners Association. The founding members recognized the need for a unified voice to represent the interests of pensioners across the twin-island nation.

1997 - First Major Achievement

ABPA successfully lobbied for improved pension indexing, ensuring that pension payments would keep pace with the cost of living. This landmark achievement benefited thousands of pensioners across the country.

2000 - Expansion of Services

The association expanded its services to include health and wellness programs, recognizing that physical and mental well-being are crucial for our senior community.

2005 - Recognition and Growth

ABPA membership grew to over 300 members, and the association gained official recognition from the government as a key stakeholder in pension-related policy discussions.

2010 - Digital Transformation

The association embraced technology, launching its first website and implementing digital communication systems to better serve members across both islands.

2015 - 20th Anniversary

ABPA celebrated its 20th anniversary with a series of events and launched new initiatives focused on financial literacy and estate planning for pensioners.

2020 - Pandemic Response

During the COVID-19 pandemic, ABPA played a crucial role in supporting vulnerable pensioners, providing information, assistance, and ensuring access to essential services.

2025 - Looking Forward

Today, ABPA continues to grow and evolve, serving over 500 members and expanding its programs to meet the changing needs of Antigua and Barbuda's pensioner community.
